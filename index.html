This website is dedicated to serial communication, the science of transmitting information from one place to another, one bit at a time. Serial communications were employed by humankind long before the advent of information theory. Instances of primitive optical and acoustic telegraphy abound. With advances in technology, namely semiconductors and electronics, it is now ubiquitous to modern life. In the space of one lifetime the speed and reliability of data transfers have become borderline magical. At the same time, legacy serial interfaces have not changed much and continue to be employed in modern, sophisticated systems precisely because, well, they simply work for simple tasks. One could imagine how the pioneers of the field would react if they could observe the most mundane of miracles such as backing up a smartphone or streaming live sports. No doubt there would be astonishment, awe and exitement due to the great achievements of mathematicians, engineers, inventors, tinkerers, hackers that have contributed to the greatness of our science and technology.

This website is a resource for learning and reference. It aims to stimulate and educate. If not interesting, at least accurate.
